# ARIA Career Operations Mode

Status: Public-safe documentation  
Purpose: Describe ARIA's progressive, workplace-style student experience without exposing private implementation details.

## What Career Operations Mode Is

Career Operations Mode is ARIA's simulated workplace experience.

Instead of seeing only a list of labs, students log in and see work appropriate to their current level. They practice like junior, intermediate, or senior IT staff under instructor supervision.

The experience is designed to feel like this:

```text
Welcome back.

You are assigned to Junior IT Operations today.
Several tickets are waiting in your queue.
Choose one to begin.
```

Students then investigate, collect evidence, ask ARIA Mentor for guidance, document their work, and submit it for review.

## Why This Matters

Many learners can watch videos or follow step-by-step labs, but real IT work requires more than following instructions.

Students need to practice:

```text
Prioritization
Professional communication
Evidence collection
Troubleshooting sequence
Documentation
Escalation judgment
Safety boundaries
Accountability
```

Career Operations Mode turns ARIA from a lab collection into a workplace simulator.

## Progressive Levels

Students progress through levels of practice.

### Junior IT Operations - L1/L2

Junior students focus on safe triage and evidence collection.

They practice:

```text
Help desk ticket handling
Endpoint checks
Basic identity and login troubleshooting
Basic Linux checks
Basic network triage
Asset documentation
Professional ticket notes
Escalation
```

Junior students are expected to prove what they observed before recommending action.

### Intermediate Infrastructure Operations - L3/L4

Intermediate students begin working with more complex operational tasks.

They practice:

```text
Approved system onboarding
Controlled remediation
Domain membership validation
Policy validation
Linux service checks
Network path analysis
Outside lab tracking
Change documentation
```

Intermediate work requires stronger pre-checks, post-checks, and rollback awareness.

### Senior Operations / Engineering - L5+

Senior students practice higher-level reasoning and leadership.

They practice:

```text
Cross-domain troubleshooting
Root cause analysis
Security triage
Change planning
Rollback planning
Automation design
Reviewing junior work
Writing runbooks
Post-incident review
```

Senior practice emphasizes risk, communication, architecture, and mentorship.

## No Skipping by Default

Students cannot skip levels by default.

Progression requires:

```text
Completed tickets
Evidence quality
Documentation quality
Safe behavior
Instructor review
Instructor approval
```

An instructor can assign stretch tickets or unlock specific practice areas when appropriate, but the system is designed to protect students from being placed into work they are not ready to perform safely.

## Practice Areas

Tickets can span several task areas:

```text
Help Desk and User Support
Identity and Access Management
Windows Endpoint Support
Linux and Systems Administration
Networking and Infrastructure
Data, Reporting, and Documentation
Software and Application Support
Security and SOC Readiness
Automation and Scripting
Asset Management
Outside Lab Practice
```

The same topic can appear at multiple levels with different expectations.

Example:

```text
Junior: Can the endpoint resolve names?
Intermediate: Why is the endpoint receiving the wrong network settings?
Senior: Is this a design or architecture issue that affects multiple teams?
```

## Instructor Control

The instructor remains central to the learning process.

Instructor responsibilities include:

```text
Approving level progression
Assigning stretch tickets
Reviewing evidence
Approving outside lab requests
Protecting sensitive or production-like assets
Confirming cleanup when temporary labs are used
Providing feedback on documentation quality
```

## Safety Boundaries

Career Operations Mode is realistic, but it is not uncontrolled.

Students operate inside defined boundaries:

```text
Training assets may be used for approved work.
Observation assets may be documented or analyzed but not changed.
Protected assets are instructor-only.
Outside labs require approval and cleanup tracking.
High-impact changes require explicit instructor approval.
```

## Student Outcomes

Students should build a portfolio of evidence that demonstrates:

```text
Realistic troubleshooting
Clear documentation
Professional communication
Safe decision-making
Technical growth
Escalation judgment
Operational readiness
```

The goal is not just completion. The goal is readiness for real IT work.
