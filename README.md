# ARIA Training Labs

ARIA is an enterprise-style IT training and practice platform designed to help aspiring, transitioning, and working technology professionals practice real operational workflows in a safe, guided environment.

This repository is the **public documentation home** for ARIA.

It contains high-level product documentation, learning model descriptions, training domain summaries, career progression plans, and sanitized examples. It intentionally does **not** contain application source code, deployment scripts, private network details, credentials, internal topology, real student records, or sensitive infrastructure information.

## What ARIA Is

ARIA is built around a simple idea:

> Students should not only study IT. They should practice working like IT employees.

ARIA combines structured labs, realistic tickets, evidence-based learning, instructor review, and AI-guided mentorship. Students practice how to investigate problems, collect proof, document findings, communicate professionally, and escalate when appropriate.

The long-term goal is to give learners a realistic bridge between classroom learning and actual IT work.

## Core Differentiator

ARIA's differentiator is not simply AI.

The differentiator is **enterprise realism**.

Students do not just click through lessons. They enter a simulated IT workplace where they receive tickets, choose work appropriate to their level, investigate systems, make safe mistakes, receive mentorship, and produce professional documentation.

Example experience:

```text
Good morning.

Four tickets came in overnight.

Which one would you like to tackle first?
```

The student chooses a ticket, investigates, gathers evidence, asks for guidance when needed, and submits work for instructor review.

## Training Domains

ARIA is organized around broad practice domains:

1. Help Desk and Ticketing
2. Identity and Access Management
3. Windows Endpoint Support
4. Linux and Systems Administration
5. Networking and Infrastructure
6. Security and SOC Readiness
7. Automation and Documentation
8. Asset Management and Operations
9. Outside Lab Practice

Each domain is designed to teach practical work habits, not just isolated commands.

## Career Operations Mode

ARIA's Career Operations Mode is designed as a progressive workplace simulation.

Students advance through levels such as:

- Junior IT Operations, L1-L2
- Intermediate Infrastructure Operations, L3-L4
- Senior Operations / Engineering, L5+

Students cannot skip levels by default. Promotion requires instructor approval and evidence that the student can work safely and professionally.

## Evidence-Based Learning

ARIA requires students to submit evidence before work is considered complete.

Evidence may include:

- Command output
- Screenshots without sensitive information
- Ticket notes
- Troubleshooting summaries
- Configuration validation
- Reflection notes
- Escalation justification

Students are trained to answer:

```text
What did you check?
What did the evidence show?
What does the evidence not prove?
What should happen next?
```

## Instructor Role

ARIA keeps the instructor in control.

The instructor reviews student work, approves progression, assigns stretch tickets, confirms outside lab boundaries, and protects the training environment from unsafe changes.
